What Is VRN?

VRN stands for Vocal Resonance Notation. Traditional music notation tells you what to sing ‚Äî pitch, rhythm, dynamics. VRN tells you how your body produces the sound ‚Äî where it resonates, how intensely, and what muscles engage.

Think of it this way: sheet music is the destination. VRN is the driving directions.

The 6 Primary Resonance Zones

Every voice uses the same six resonating chambers. VRN gives each one a letter.

[C]
Chest
Sternum, ribs, lower torso
[H]
Head
Skull, forehead, crown
[N]
Nasal / Mask
Nose bridge, cheekbones, sinuses
[O]
Oral
Mouth cavity, palate, tongue
[P]
Pharyngeal
Throat space, open pharynx
[L]
Low Body
Abdomen, lower back, pelvic floor

Intensity Scale

Each zone gets an intensity level. More + signs = stronger engagement. Think of it like weight at the gym ‚Äî [C+] is a warm-up set, [C+++] is a max-effort lift.

++

Why VRN Didn't Exist Before

Western music notation evolved over centuries to capture pitch, rhythm, and dynamics ‚Äî the what of music. But it never addressed the how of vocal production.

Voice teaching has been an oral tradition for 400+ years. Teachers demonstrate, students imitate. Knowledge passes through metaphor and physical proximity. Nothing gets written down.

VRN does for vocal production what the International Phonetic Alphabet did for phonetics ‚Äî creates a standardized written system for something that was previously passed down by ear and touch.

Developed for COSMOS the OPERA ‚Äî a full-scale opera requiring precise vocal production specifications across an entire cast ‚Äî VRN emerged from the need to write down what no notation system could capture.
